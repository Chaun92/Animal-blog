Red Fox

Foxes: Cunning and Diverse Creatures Foxes are clever and adaptable animals that belong to the same family as dogs, wolves, and jackals. There are over 20 species of foxes, ranging from the tiny fennec fox to the large red fox, which can be found on every continent except Antarctica and South America.

  • Scientific Name : Vulpes vulpes
  • Average Length:14-20 inches tall (shoulder height) and 18-35 inches long
  • Average Lifespan:3-13 years depending on type of fox and living conditions
  • Habitat: Their preferred habitats are mixed landscapes

Their preferred habitats are mixed landscapes, but they live in environments ranging from Arctic tundra to arid desert. Red foxes adapt very well to human presence, thriving in areas with farmland and woods, and populations can be found in many large cities and suburbs.
Widely held as a symbol of animal cunning, the red fox is the subject of considerable folklore. The red fox has the largest natural distribution of any land mammal except human beings. In the Old World it ranges over virtually all of Europe, temperate Asia, and northern Africa, and in the New World it inhabits most of North America.
